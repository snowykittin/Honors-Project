    Assignment Details

    The deliverable for this assignment involved creating a Heuristic Analysis. A heuristic analysis is a report documenting how a website matches up to heuristics. Heuristics are mental short-cuts, a set of already existing connections that make navigating the world and making judgements much easier. These heuristics exist all around us in terms of computing. An example of such is the home button: almost everybody now has the heuristic that they expect a home button to be on the bottom center of a phone screen. Taking the time to have a heuristic analysis of a website conducted can allow for web masters to understand how their website might be making sense to them, but not to the rest of their customers. It gives the chance to identify shortcomings in the design on an organizational level. For this assignment, students were tasked with picking a website, and reporting on 15-20 findings.

    Reflection

    I definitely had the most fun with this assignment. Having taken AP Psychology in high school, I already had an idea on that heuristics were. Applying that to the computing space opened up my eyes to just how many expectations people have when it comes to a program, a lot of which people don’t understand. A lot of the time, the reasons we find something to be ‘unaesthetically pleasing’ really comes down to the fact that it doesn’t fit any heuristics that we currently have. I chose to evaluate Craigslist, and given the age of the site, I knew that there would be quite a few findings. I was pleasantly surprised by just how much I found, though, and how little of those actually happened to deal with aesthetics. Such a well-known website actually had quite a significant amount of heuristic findings that had room for improvement. For its core purpose, craigslist serves it rather well, but a downside is that the overall website had attempted to expand into categories that it really didn’t need to. An example of this was their blog. For a website that has come to be known around selling items, having a blog just doesn’t make much sense.

    If I did this assignment again, I certainly still would have chosen Craigslist as my website. There was a lot of really good insights to take into account when I made my report, and I actually had a lot of fun. I had to think critically when finding things that seemed off to me. Trying my best when making recommendations, that was certainly my weakest point. Being able to pinpoint what all is wrong is easy, but giving solutions to fix it is what is much harder. I would work a lot more on giving better recommendations, and ideally, proposing 2-3 solutions for each problem that would be ranked from “easiest” to “most difficult.” I also would have really liked to try to pinpoint more findings on the pages that people are more familiar with. One of the uses of Craigslist that I am most familiar with is selling vehicles. The way those pages are set up is quite a bit different than a lot of the rest of the site, and while evaluating it as a whole certainly is important, focusing on the main purpose of the website would have been more beneficial. Of course, Craigslist probably won’t ever see my heuristic evaluation, but in attempting to mock real life, focusing on the primary objective of the website would have been a much better decision instead of saving it to be the couple of end findings.

    Conducting a heuristic analysis is something that I actually don’t see myself doing in my professional career. Considering that I personally don’t find myself in a UX researcher role, it’s unlikely I’ll end up doing one. However, being able to complete it is still important. It gave me good insight into expectations that users have, as well as being able to detail solutions to problems that may occur. Given that I would ideally like to end up in some type of leadership position, that is often where people tend to ask for suggestions. If I am able to find out just what it is that doesn’t seem right to me, that just makes me critical. But being able to actually give reasons why it’s a problem, as well as the suggestions to fix them, changes it from just simply being a negative bit of feedback into a true suggestion people will take into account. Not a lot of people like to hear that they’ve made a mistake, and I am no stranger to that type of pitfall. However, most of us, hopefully all of us, are human. Imperfection is part of our nature, but striving to be better is an important quality that I hope to continue to push myself and other people to delve into.
