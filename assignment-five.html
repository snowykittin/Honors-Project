    Assignment Details

    This assignment takes everything into account from past assignments to put together a final UI. While not programmed, it serves as a simulation to the final product. In creating this final design, thinking of all recommendations gathered was extremely important. Students had to factor in feedback on their wireframes of Assignment 2, the original scenario that they had proposed in Assignment 1, any heuristics they may catch from having done Assignment 3, and most importantly, the feedback from users in Assignment 4. All of this information is important in creating the final design, as it shows they are capable of understanding feedback and implementing it into a design. No design is ever perfect, and as such, changes will be made. Another requirement that had to be met was creating an error screen. In life, nobody will ever follow the exact path that a programmer expects them to. As such, having error cases to handle when something goes awry is critical to ensure that users are able to fulfill heuristics of error prevention and error handling. People are not stupid, and as such, if you give them a problem, they will naturally want to find the solution. Overall, this assignment is to serve as a 'final' for the course, bringing everything together into one final product.

    Reflection

    Perhaps it was due to a few absences, but this was the assignment I felt had the least connection to the rest of the course. It certainly was a logical choice for the process we took, as the course essentially took students through the entire design process, just from a UX researcher’s perspective. However, the course mainly drove students to look at things from a UX perspective, and I feel that making a final design really don’t accomplish that. It seems much more like something intended for software designers instead of a UX researcher. I understand that it came from the idea of being able to take all of the feedback that was gained from the other assignments and turn the kiosk into something better, but again, it just doesn’t seem like much of a UX researcher’s thing to do. If anything, I certainly would have expected having to do a paper that was a massive proposal document to make the changes that were suggested along the way. Alongside making those suggestions, there would have to be the evidence from each assignment to support it along the way. Pulling things in like heuristics of the wireframe, the suggestions from the usability test, and even suggestions that were made simply from revisiting the design after having some time away from it. From my perspective, that would be much more applicable to a UX researcher than actually creating something off of those suggestions. After all, at least to my knowledge, UX researchers usually aren’t the ones who have to program the final product.

    When it comes to improving past assignments, this is the one that I certainly have the most amount of improvement that I could have made. Should I ever have the chance to redo this assignment, I’d essentially start from scratch. There is one massive pitfall that I have as a student, and even as a person: I tend to get rather lazy when I’m comfortable with the material. Many excuses come to mind when I think about the “why” behind it all. I think of things like having a grade buffer so I can afford to slack of a little bit, I didn’t have the time to complete it, I didn’t exactly know what to do. At the heart of all of these though is simply the fact that I get a bit lazy when I feel confident in knowing what I’m doing, and that was exactly what had happened with this assignment. I certainly could have done a much better job of prototyping the final UI from the wireframe, even with the limitations that Whimsical had. If anything, I feel like the kiosk is still in a beta phase, rather than a final UI design. I also would have taken the time to think the overall design out much more extensively. One thing that I did do for this assignment that I will certainly be attempting to do when referencing existing content in the future is I looked up the media package for the Indiana Convention Center. Being able to take resources from the already-existing source and implement them into a kiosk for that place is an important skill to have for a programmer, as more often than not, the programmer isn’t going to be the one actually designing the kiosk. They’re just going to program it and make it come to life.

    If there was any assignment that basically acted as a blanket statement to what I’m likely going to be doing for the rest of my life, this is it. As of this present moment in time, I see myself getting a job on the software programming side of things. That means taking already existing content, tweaking it to fit a set of objectives or suggestions, and delivering a final product. This also means it is the assignment that I should have put the most effort into, as it would make for a great portfolio item. However, given that I am very aware that I fell short of doing that for this particular assignment, it also taught me one of the largest lessons for this course. Funnily enough, it also fits in with one of the heuristics and lessons that I learned about in the class as well: Humans are naturally lazy. We want to take the least amount of steps and time to get something done, and we’re rather determined to do just that. Programmers are actually a testament to that fact, as there’s a reason that we joke our jobs are essentially done through “Ctrl+C” and “Ctrl+V.” This assignment was both good and bad for me for another of reasons, but overall, I still appreciate actually being able to bring together everything that I had done over the rest of the semester.
